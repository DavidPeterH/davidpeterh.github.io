The Militiaman

Documentary short film (32 minutes) - premieres 2021.

In the hills of rural Pennsylvania, the leader of a local militia must prepare his men for the changing landscape of 2020 while wrestling with his own conscience.

For over ten years, 48-year-old Iraqi War Veteran and machinist Christian Yingling has commanded a troop of private militiamen and women concerned with the government’s infringement on their constitutional rights. The group practices paramilitary drills, stockpiles food and ammo, and attends gun rights rallies in preparation for a doomsday scenario. Now that a worldwide pandemic has hit, followed by a summer of racial injustice protests and a Presidential election like no other, Christian—out of work and nearly out of money—must confront his allegiance and choose to act or not.

American Wrestler (working title)

Documentary short film - in production.

An injured wrestler wants to leave his dead-end pizza delivery job to become the next WWE superstar. Stephen Andretta powerslams, piledrives and superkicks his way through training at a small Long Island gym, while looking for the gig that will finally land him in the big leagues. The competition is high and the risk of further injury increasing, but Even Stephen believes he has what it takes to make it all the way.

About David Peter

David Peter is a documentary filmmaker and journalist from Denmark, currently based in New York. He has worked in post-conflict zones worldwide and has worked and lived in Africa, the Middle East, Asia, and Europe. David Peter is an M.S. candidate in Journalism (Documentary Specialization) from Columbia Journalism School and holds an M.A. in Conflict, Security, and Development from the University of Bradford.
